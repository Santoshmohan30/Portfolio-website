What I Built and Learned This Week

Week 12 • March 20, 2026

What I worked on

This week I focused on tightening the connection between backend architecture and model-driven workflow design. That included thinking through cleaner API boundaries for document processing work and making the structure of an agentic retrieval system easier to reason about.

Problem faced

The recurring challenge was not feature count. It was keeping systems understandable as they gained more moving parts. Once retrieval, validation, storage, and orchestration begin to interact, weak boundaries start creating debugging cost very quickly.

What I tried

I first explored simpler all-in-one flow designs because they were fast to prototype. That helped me test direction, but it also made me notice how quickly logic becomes entangled when too much sits in one layer.

What failed

Some initial structure assumed that downstream validation could be added later without changing the rest of the pipeline. In practice, that assumption was weak. Once a system needs trust and traceability, validation has to shape the architecture from the beginning.

What worked

Breaking responsibilities into clearer stages improved both implementation clarity and future maintainability. The strongest progress came from defining interfaces and expected outputs before optimizing details.

Key takeaways

The best weekly progress often comes from reducing ambiguity, not adding more code. When a pipeline becomes easier to inspect, the next round of improvement becomes much more focused.

Why it matters for industry / engineering / ML / science

Teams need engineers who can keep complexity legible while a system grows. That is true for ML products, backend services, and research pipelines alike, and it is one of the habits I’m intentionally strengthening every week.

Weekly logs are designed to show iteration, not perfection.
